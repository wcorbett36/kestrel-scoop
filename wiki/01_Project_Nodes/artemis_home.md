# artemis_home

## Summary
Artemis Home is a comprehensive platform for publishing research and artifacts, focusing on content management, distribution, and media handling. It includes detailed guidelines for authoring, publishing, and syndicating content across various channels, with a strategic focus on trust, semantics, middleware, platform decisions, and infrastructure. The platform aims to establish a robust framework for trustworthy operations by addressing policy, provenance, boundaries, runtime evidence, and observability.

## Strategic Gap Analysis
The artifact layer is mostly conceptual and lacks artifacts at E3 level. The media workflow demo is useful internally but should not be included in the public roadmap unless rewritten as a real editorial note. There is a need for more detailed guidelines on content syndication and distribution across various channels. The platform is intentionally lightweight and serves as a vocabulary and design substrate for later implementation work. There are no explicit local gaps or risks mentioned in the content itself, but further research and development are needed to address the strategic gaps in trust evaluation, policy enforcement, and error handling. The project identifies a need for explicit trust decisions, structured evidence, and policy decision and enforcement separation. It also highlights the importance of observability and control surfaces to ensure that systems are explainable and controllable. Additionally, there is a gap in the current implementation regarding the enforcement of runtime invariants and the exchange of context between different components.
